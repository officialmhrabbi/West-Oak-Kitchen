How It Began

Rooted in Tradition,
Refined by Passion

Since 2020, West Oak Kitchen has been a destination for those who believe that great food is more than sustenance — it is a language, a ritual, and a bridge between people.

Born from a deep respect for seasonal ingredients and artisanal technique, our kitchen bridges the flavors of heritage and the precision of modern culinary craft. Every plate is a conversation between the past and the present.

What started as a small neighbourhood table in the West Village has grown into one of New York's most quietly celebrated dining rooms — a place where regulars return not just for the food, but for the feeling.

The Hand Behind the Plate

Chef
Marcus Elliot

With over two decades in kitchens across Paris, Tokyo, and New York, Marcus brings a quiet authority to every dish — one built on discipline, curiosity, and a reverence for the seasons.

Trained under Alain Ducasse and later at Narisawa in Tokyo, Marcus developed a philosophy that defies easy labels. For him, cooking is an act of listening — to the ingredient, to the diner, to the moment.

"I don't cook to impress. I cook to connect. The finest meal is the one a guest remembers not for its technique, but for how it made them feel."

Marcus Elliot Executive Chef & Co-Founder
